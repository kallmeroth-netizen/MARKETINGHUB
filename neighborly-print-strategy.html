■ As-needed · Per event · Per location
Special Messaging & Pop-Up Standard
One postcard + one poster per location, per pop-up or special campaign. A fixed, repeatable brief — keeps production predictable and budgetable even when events aren't planned far out. No exceptions to the 1+1 rule.

Programme detail
GGeneric card — what goes on it
  • Side A: Seasonal message + one clear CTA
  • Side B: Rotating special, new item, or brand moment
  • Same design both locations, separate print runs
  • Qty: ~10–12 weeks of takeout bag volume per location
CCatering card — what goes on it
  • Side A: Catering pitch — groups, corporate, events, custom menus
  • Side B: Contact, ordering link, socials, minimums
  • Semi-evergreen copy — no hard expiry dates
  • Qty: 8–10 weeks of inquiry + delivery volume per location
PPop-up card — what goes on it
  • Side A: Event date, name, what to expect, RSVP link
  • Side B: Permanent brand block — address, hours, socials, order link
  • Can be location-specific if event is single-site
  • Qty: 1–2 weeks of in-bag distribution around event

Production notes

✓Keeping it streamlined

  • Generic card quarterly = always something fresh in bag, never stale, no guesswork
  • Poster always mirrors the active postcard — one brief, two pieces, every time
  • Catering cards are semi-evergreen — two designs carry the full year
  • Catering cards go with catering only — not competing in the takeout bag
  • Pop-up standard (1+1 per location) makes event print predictable and budgetable
  • Both locations run separately so quantities and copy tweaks can differ

→Briefing & lead times

  • Brief generic + catering items 6–8 weeks before the quarter start date
  • Q1 brief: November 15  ·  Q2: February 15  ·  Q3: May 15  ·  Q4: August 15
  • Pop-up cards briefed minimum 3 weeks before in-location need date
  • Send-to-print deadline = 10 days before any in-bag or in-location need date
  • Both locations confirm quantities before each print run is placed
  • Artwork approval required in the print tracker before send-to-print is checked
