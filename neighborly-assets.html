06 Voice

Thoughtful, Friendly, Informative

Warm, plainspoken, never saleslike.

Our Voice
Like a good neighbor — clear, kind, and never trying too hard.
Do

Write short. Say "we" and "you." Use real words real people use at a dinner table.

Do

Lead with the food, the people, and the place. Specifics over superlatives.

Don't

Don't use slang or overly trendy words.

Don't

Don't oversell, or hedge.

07 Imagery

Photography

A brand-approved library. Pull from this set whenever possible.

Brand Library

Photography by Brand

The full image archive — food, people, neighborhood, and hospitality. Organized by brand.

Open Library
Natural Light

Natural sunlight where possible. Soft shadows, real ambient color. Avoid heavy flash or hard direction.

Lived-In Frames

Show the food and the room as they are. Crumbs, hands, half-poured drinks.

People in Frame

Hospitality is the point. When practical, include the chef, the staff, or the guest enjoying the food.

No Heavy Filters

Color-correct for accuracy, not vibe. Avoid presets that warp skin tones or oversaturate produce.
