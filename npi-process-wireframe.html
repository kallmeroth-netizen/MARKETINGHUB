Marketplace Operations

New Product Introduction — How It Works

Every brand we approve becomes a launch that moves through six stages, from sample to shelf. Each stage is owned by one team, and the moment a team finishes its checklist, the system automatically emails the next team that it's their turn — no chasing, no spreadsheets, no "who owns this?" A launch can't move forward until the prior stage is 100% complete.

The pipeline · sample → shelf
STAGE 1–2
Marketplace

Discovery & Sample Review

  • Request & receive samples
  • Tasting + taste approval
  • Margin & assortment approval
  • Collect brand assets
✉On Sample approved → emails Procurement
STAGE 3
Procurement

Commercial Setup

  • Vendor setup, W9, insurance
  • Terms & cost sheet
  • Case pack / MOQ
  • Secure free first fill
✉On First fill secured → emails Commissary
STAGE 4
Commissary

Supply Chain

  • Create warehouse SKU
  • Receiving setup & SOP
  • Storage location
  • Reorder point
✉On SKU created → emails Operations
STAGE 5
Operations

Retail Readiness

  • Create POS / Toast item
  • Shelf label
  • Add to order guide
  • Establish pars
✉On Pars established → emails Marketing
STAGE 6
Marketing

Launch Readiness

  • Product photography
  • Product story / description
  • Print shelf labels
  • Schedule social & email
✉On Assets done → emails Store Ops
STAGE 7 · LIVE
Store Ops

Store Launch

  • Receive & merchandise
  • Install shelf tags
  • Train team
  • Verify launch
✉On Go Live → emails Marketplace + starts 30-day review

Why this beats the spreadsheet

Nothing slips. A launch can't advance until the owning team's checklist hits 100% — built-in stage gates.

Everyone owns their lane. Each team has a live dashboard of only their open tasks across every launch.

Hands-off handoffs. Every email is automatic and Neighborly-branded — the next team is notified the second they're up.

Neighborly Marketplace · NPI — live pipeline in the team hub. Automated, branded notifications across Marketplace · Procurement · Commissary · Operations · Marketing · Store Ops.
